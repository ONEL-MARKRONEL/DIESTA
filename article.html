Understanding Plagiarism

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or words without giving proper credit. It is considered unethical and can have serious consequences in academic, professional, and creative settings.

Why is Plagiarism a Problem?

Plagiarism undermines trust, disrespects the original creator, and can result in legal penalties or damage to one's reputation. In academic environments, it can lead to expulsion or failing grades.

How to Avoid Plagiarism

  • Use Proper Citations: Always give credit to the original author when using their work.
  • Paraphrase: Restate ideas in your own words while still acknowledging the source.
  • Quote: Use quotation marks for exact phrases or sentences and include a citation.
  • Understand Copyright: Familiarize yourself with copyright laws and fair use guidelines.
  • Use Plagiarism Detection Tools: Tools like Turnitin or Grammarly can help ensure your work is original.
